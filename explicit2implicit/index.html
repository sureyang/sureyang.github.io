From explicit to implicit: Investigate video content change for robot imitation learning

Shuo Yang, Wei Zhang, Weizhi Lu, Hesheng Wang, and Yibin Li

Abstract

Demonstration understanding is the vital component for robot imitation learning. In this article, we thoroughly investigate the change-based representation of demonstration and utilize the representation to build imitation learning system in explicit and implicit ways. First, we propose a novel representation named content change map and utilize it to build imitation learning system in explicit way. To build a more "human-like" imitation learning system, we try to extend the change-based representation from image-level to feature-level and build full imitation learning system by implicit method. We conduct exhaustive experiments for our explicit and implicit methods, experimental results verify the effectiveness and advantages of our two methods. Finally, we conduct comparative experimental study between the two methods and the results demonstrate the superiority of implicit way for robot imitation learning.
